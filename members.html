Michele
CASTELLI

Art Historian

Michele Castelli (Naples, 2000) earned his bachelor’s degree in Archaeology, Art History, and Cultural Heritage Studies (summa cum laude) from the University of Naples, Federico II. He is currently pursuing a master’s degree in Arts, Museology, and Curatorship at the University of Bologna (UNIBO). His academic interests span early to late modern art history (15th to 19th century), philosophy, and European history. His current research focuses on the relationship between artists and patrons during the Renaissance, with a particular emphasis on the Flemish region.

Nigar
ALIZADA

Author

Nigar Alizada, a graduate in Art History from Mimar Sinan Fine Arts University specializing in feminist art, wrote her thesis on gender themes in the works of Gülsün Karamustafa. She enhanced her studies through an Erasmus+ exchange at Duisburg-Essen University and contributed to a seminar on cultural heritage protection. Continuing her education, she pursued a master’s degree at Tor Vergata University in Rome, focusing on various artistic styles and exhibition aesthetics. Nigar gained practical experience at a contemporary art museum in Istanbul, where she developed skills in art interpretation and visitor engagement. She is fluent in Turkish and Azerbaijani, proficient in English, and has a working knowledge of Italian and Russian, facilitating her ability to collaborate internationally.

Asia
GRAZIANO

Author

Asia Graziano is an art historian with degrees in Cultural Heritage and Visual Arts, currently working as an editor and contributor for art publishers and magazines. She made a notable contribution by publishing a document attributing a wooden sculpture of Saint Peter Martyr to Donatello and co-curating a major monograph on Artemisia Gentileschi with prominent scholars. Graziano’s research focuses on Italian Renaissance and Baroque art, with extensive work on women's artistic contributions from the Middle Ages to the present.

Martina
Di Girolamo

Author

Martina Di Girolamo, originally from Foggia, holds a bachelor's degree in New Technologies of Art and a specialization in Art Communication and Education from the Academy of Fine Arts in Rome. Her engagement with the art world intensified through her work with the curatorial collective SENZASPAZIO on a temporary exhibition project. Focusing on modern and contemporary art, particularly from the late 19th century onward, Martina is dedicated to curating and sharing artistic knowledge. She has also written theses on post-production as an artistic practice, examining the reworking of images online, and on the artistic duo Eva and Franco Mattes.
