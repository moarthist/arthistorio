The talented individuals who make it all possible

Mahmoud MOHAMMADI

Founder

Born on December 16, 1995 in Iran, Mahmoud Mohammadi is a multifaceted art historian, curator, and digital content producer. He has a solid academic background and holds a Master of Art History of Iran from Isfahan University of Art. He is currently studying another Master's program in Visual Arts at the University of Bologna, Italy, majoring in Museology and Curatorship, and joined Erasmus exchange program at the University of Liège, Belgium. He is also a graduate of the highly revered Berlin University of the Arts (UDK), with studies in film and video art editing. Trained in Persian manuscriptology, documentary research, and visual storytelling, he is fluent in Persian, English, French.

Desiré FERRIERO

Italian Language and Literature

She attended high school focusing on classical studies, where she studied Italian, Latin, and ancient Greek, igniting her passion for Italian literature. After graduating in 2021, she moved to Bologna to pursue a bachelor's degree in literature, emphasizing classical studies. Through her exploration of ancient cultures, she became convinced that understanding the past is crucial for interpreting the present.

Michele CASTELLI

Art Historian

Michele Castelli (Naples, 2000) earned his bachelor's degree in Archaeology, Art History, and Cultural Heritage Studies (summa cum laude) from the University of Naples, Federico II. He is currently pursuing a master's degree in Arts, Museology, and Curatorship at the University of Bologna (UNIBO). His academic interests span early to late modern art history (15th to 19th century), philosophy, and European history.

Nigar ALIZADA

Author

Nigar Alizada, a graduate in Art History from Mimar Sinan Fine Arts University specializing in feminist art, wrote her thesis on gender themes in the works of Gülsün Karamustafa. She enhanced her studies through an Erasmus+ exchange at Duisburg-Essen University and contributed to a seminar on cultural heritage protection.

Asia GRAZIANO

Author

Asia Graziano is an art historian with degrees in Cultural Heritage and Visual Arts, currently working as an editor and contributor for art publishers and magazines. She made a notable contribution by publishing a document attributing a wooden sculpture of Saint Peter Martyr to Donatello and co-curating a major monograph on Artemisia Gentileschi with prominent scholars.

Martina Di Girolamo

Author

Martina Di Girolamo, originally from Foggia, holds a bachelor's degree in New Technologies of Art and a specialization in Art Communication and Education from the Academy of Fine Arts in Rome. Her engagement with the art world intensified through her work with the curatorial collective SENZASPAZIO on a temporary exhibition project.
